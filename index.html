Research

With MIT PRIMES, I am currently conducting research in the realm of distributed systems. I am a member of the D.O.C.C. lab, a research group at Tufts focusing on the diagnosis, observability, and configuration of cloud systems.

Abstract

Long tail latencies continue to hold back the performance of microservices, especially at scale. Timeouts are important in determining whether a request has succeeded or failed, yet there has not been a significant volume of work so far to find an optimal timeout. To mitigate long wait times, we propose many approaches to determine the optimal amount of time to wait before deciding that a request will never come back and sending another one (specifically with idempotent APIs: making multiple requests to an API will have the same behavior as a single request). We condense our methods in a system called ALTO (ALTO Analyzes Latencies for Timeout Optimization). In order to evaluate these approaches, a new microservice testbed was developed with simplicity, robustness, and comprehensibility in mind, rather than solely performance. In order to evaluate ALTO, we will model realistic changes in the testbed, and verify that ALTO modifies timeouts appropriately in a timely manner.
